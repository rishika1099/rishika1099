I added one more variable to a Cox model and watched chemotherapy flip from
helping patients to seemingly harming them. The model wasn't broken, I was.

That one variable was a collider, and conditioning on it opened a fake path
between treatment and outcome. This post is about why the causal structure of
your variables matters far more than how many predictors you throw in, and how
a cancer trial taught me to ask the right question before trusting the answer.
